About

I'm a game developer and educator based in London. I build games for universities, museums and edtech teams — the kind of work where the point isn't only that it's fun, but that it does something real: a study that runs, a concept that finally lands, a learner who gets it.

Before going freelance I spent a year at Supersolid keeping live mobile games stable for over a hundred thousand daily players, so I understand production and release, not only prototypes. I teach game design at the National Film and Television School, which means I can shape what to build and explain it clearly to non-technical stakeholders — not just write the code. My way of working is link-making: pulling the right mechanic, system or medium toward the story you're trying to tell.
